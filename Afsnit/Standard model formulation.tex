\section{Baseline Model}

\paragraph{Preferences}

Following \textcite{Keane2016}, households derive utility from consumption and disutility from labour supply. Period utility is given by

\[
U_t =
\frac{c_t^{1+\eta}}{1+\eta}
-
\beta(n_t)\frac{h_t^{1+\gamma}}{1+\gamma},
\]

where $c_t$ denotes consumption and $h_t$ hours worked. $\eta$ and $\gamma$ determine the curvature of utility from consumption and the disutility of labour. $\beta(n_t)$ scales the disutility of labour depending if children are present.

\paragraph{Dynamic Optimization Problem}

Households choose consumption and labour supply to maximize expected lifetime utility. The recursive problem can be written as

\[
V_t(n_t,a_t,k_t)
=
\max_{c_t,h_t}
\left\{
\frac{c_t^{1+\eta}}{1+\eta}
-
\beta(n_t)\frac{h_t^{1+\gamma}}{1+\gamma}
+
\rho \mathbb{E}_t
\left[
V_{t+1}(n_{t+1},a_{t+1},k_{t+1})
\right]
\right\},
\]

where $a_t$ denotes assets, $k_t$ human capital, and $\rho$ the discount factor.

\paragraph{The internals of the Bellman equation}

Fertility evolves according to a stochastic process

\[
n_{t+1} =
\begin{cases}
n_t + 1 & \text{with probability } p(n_t) \\
n_t & \text{with probability } 1 - p(n_t)
\end{cases}
\]

Assets evolve according to the household's budget constraint

\[
a_{t+1} = (1+r)(a_t + (1-\tau)w_t h_t - c_t),
\]

where $\tau$ is the labour income tax rate. Human capital accumulates through labour supply

\[
k_{t+1} = k_t + h_t.
\]

We let the disutilty of children be given by the formulation

\[\beta(n_t)=\beta_0+\beta_1\cdot n_t\]

Since we will solve by backwards induction, we last period maximization problem will be the maximisation of the original period utility given in \cite{Keane2016}

\[V_{T}(n_T,a_T,k_T)=\max_{h_{T}}\frac{c_{T}^{1+\eta}}{1+\eta}-\beta(n_T)\frac{h_{T}^{1+\gamma}}{1+\gamma} \quad \text{s.t} \quad 
c_T = a_{T}+(1-\tau)w_{T}h_{T}.
\]
